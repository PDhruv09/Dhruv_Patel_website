Does Urbanization effect Power Outage?

"Does Urbanization Affect Power Outages?" is an analytical project designed for the DSC 80 course at UCSD. The study examines major power outages in the United States from January 2000 to July 2016, utilizing data from Purdue University's Laboratory for Advancing Sustainable Critical Infrastructure. The analysis investigates whether the duration of power outages differs significantly between states with high urbanization levels and states with low urbanization levels. Using data columns like OUTAGE.DURATION and POPPCT_URBAN (percentage of urban population), the project employs a permutation test with 10,000 simulations to test the hypothesis. By uncovering patterns in outage duration relative to urbanization, the findings aim to provide insights for policymakers, utility companies, and the public to improve infrastructure planning and disaster preparedness.
